Guide to Writing Learning Objectives

A learning objective is a clear and specific statement of measurable student behavior. The objective needs to be clear and specific so that students understand what they should be able to do after a session. The objective should be measurable to allow for assessment of whether a student has met the objective.

Learning Objective vs. Learning Outcome

Learning objectives are often confused for learning outcomes, but there are several important differences. A learning outcome is a broad goal of what a student should be able to do at the end of a session or course. They are usually written in general terms, and consequently are often not measurable or assessible. In contrast, learning objectives are written in specific terms and allow for assessment of whether the objective has been met. Learning outcomes are supported and defined by one or more learning objectives.
